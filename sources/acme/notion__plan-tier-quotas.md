This document defines the hard and soft limits for each Acme plan tier. These limits are enforced by the `billing-gateway` service and mirrored in the warehouse for reporting. For the pricing associated with these tiers, see `notion__pricing-tiers.md`.

## Quota Matrix

| Feature | Free | Pro | Business | Enterprise |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Monthly Workflow Runs** | 100 | 10,000 | 100,000 | Unlimited |
| **Max Active Workflows** | 2 | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |

| **Concurrency Limit** | 1 | 5 | 20 | Custom (Default 50) |

## Enforcement Policies

### 1. Workflow Run Quotas (Monthly)
*   **Free Tier**: Hard enforcement. Once a customer hits 100 runs, all subsequent triggers (webhook, schedule, or manual) return a `QUOTA_EXCEEDED` error. Workflows are auto-paused until the first day of the next calendar month.
*   **Pro & Business**: Soft enforcement with notification. We do **not** currently kill runs for paying customers who exceed 10k/100k limits. Instead, the system triggers an internal alert to the assigned AE or the growth team.
*   **Enterprise**: No cap. Usage is monitored for infrastructure capacity planning only.

### 2. Seat Count Limits
*   **Pro**: Capped at 49 seats. At 50 seats, the UI forces a "Contact Sales" modal to transition the account to Business.
*   **Business**: Minimum 50 seat commitment. We occasionally allow "under-seating" (e.g., 30 seats) for strategic MM accounts, but this requires @rachel.stein approval. See `gong__discovery__cust000412-drag-industries.md` for a recent exception.

## Upgrade Triggers & AE Alerts

We use a "90% Utilization" threshold to trigger expansion and upgrade conversations. These alerts are generated by `dbt__model__workflow_runs_daily.md` and routed via the `acme-growth-bot`.

*   **Pro → Business Trigger**: A Pro customer exceeding 9,000 runs in a rolling 30-day window OR reaching 40 seats.
*   **Business → Enterprise Trigger**: A Business customer exceeding 90,000 runs in a rolling 30-day window OR requesting SCIM/Advanced RBAC features.

> **Note from @jorge.martinez**: Sales Ops is currently tracking "Quota Burn Rate" in Salesforce to predict which accounts will hit their 90% trigger mid-quarter. If you see an Opportunity created with the source `usage_expansion`, this is the trigger that fired it.

## Technical Implementation

Usage is tracked in real-time via Redis counters in the production cluster. Every 15 minutes, these counts are flushed to the product DB and subsequently ingested into the warehouse.

*   **Source Table**: `fact_workflow_runs`
*   **Rollup Model**: `dbt__model__workflow_runs_daily.md`
*   **Enforcement Service**: `billing-gateway` (owned by @tomas.vega)

## FAQ

**Q: Do failed runs count against the quota?**
Yes. Any run that is successfully triggered and enters the `started` state counts toward the monthly quota, regardless of whether it ends in `success` or `error`.

**Q: Can a customer purchase "Overage Packs"?**
Not currently. We prefer to push customers to the next tier rather than managing granular overage billing. This is a frequent request from the Sales team that @sam.reyes has explicitly tabled for 2026.

**Q: How is "Active Workflow" defined for the Free tier?**
An "Active" workflow is any workflow where the `is_enabled` toggle is set to TRUE. Free users can have 50 drafts, but only 2 can be enabled to listen for triggers at any given time.
